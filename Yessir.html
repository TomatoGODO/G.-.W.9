In days of yore, when sun and moon did wend,

Life's purpose sought, a quest without end

Some deem it gold, a worldly pursuit,

Yet others find purpose in nature's repute.



For in the meadow, where flowers fair,

Life's meaning blooms, beyond compare

To merchants, coin is the eternal guide,

While scholars seek wisdom in books beside.



Yet in the shadows, there exists a few,

Who glimpse beyond the ordinary view.

Like stargazers, who, in the night's embrace,

See purpose in the cosmos, vast and grace.



The farmer tills, a purpose in the soil,

A life's fulfillment, despite turmoil.

To sailors, purpose lies in the restless sea,

Endless horizons where destiny may be.



So, in life's tapestry, woven and spun,

Each thread a purpose, distinct, yet one.

For some, mere mortals, the surface unfolds,

Yet seers divine, where truth quietly holds.
